MUMBAI

MASALA LIBRARAY,BANDRA EAST

Masala Library was one of the very first places to reinvent Indian food, and is a pioneer when it comes to showing the world what Modern Indian food looks like. It’s quite bougie, so perfect for celebrations! After your hearty meal, wash it all down with a Curry Leaf Martini. Must Try:The iconic Jalebi Caviar, the Crab Bhurji, the Paneer Burrata Lababdar, and the Kundapur Chicken.

SPICEKLUB,LOWER PAREL

SpiceKlub is another iconic spot that’s bringing creativity to the Indian food scene in Mumbai. This spot is uniquely fully vegetarian, so if you’re with a group that’s particular about these things, this is your spot!Must Try: You’ve got to try the Vegetable and Beetroot Spheres, the Masala Minestrone Soup, the popular Pav Bhaji Fondue and the test-tube Pani Puri!

Aaswad, Dadar West

London's Foodie Hub Global Awards has awarded Aaswad's Misal as the best vegetarian food in the world, in the past. Aaswad regularly features on lists that discuss the best places in the world to catch vegetarian food, and authentic Maharashtrian food.

PRATAP THE DHABA,OSHIWARA

Locals know about how amazing the food here is. We all love the fried garlic that comes as a side, the paan shot that's the perfect palate cleanser, and the experimental Vodka Pani Puri. Known for their Mughlai food, fair warning, the food is ultra indulgent, and everything is makkhan-laden and calorie-unfriendly, but worth it.Must Try: We love their Sarson Ka Saag and Makai ki Roti, and their Butter Chicken.

JAMAVAR,THE LEELA,ANDHERI EAST

Jamavar seeks to dish up food that the royals of yore ate, using traditional recipes and cooking techniques. With silver cutlery and crockery, and an ambiance that is super regal. Must Try: Dum biryani and kebabs

HARYANA

AMRIK SUKHDEV,MRUTHAL

Amrik Sukhdev was initially created in 1956 with the idea to cater to the needs of truck drivers. Today, the eatery is known for serving a wide range of guests who can enjoy north and south Indian delicacies, along with Chinese and continental cuisine.Recommended dishes:Famous stuffed parathas, butter chicken, and dal makhani. And don't forget to end your meal with their yummy lassi!

BIKANERWALA,GURUGRAM

Bikanervala traces its humble beginnings to 1950 when two brothers decided to open a shop selling sweets. Over the years, Bikanervala gained incredible success, opened shops across India and a few other countries, and became a synonym for Indian sweets and snacks.Recommended dishes:Sure! Bikanervala is a great place to try some delicious Indian dishes. Here are a few recommendations: their Raj Kachori, Chole Bhature, Paneer Tikka, and their mouthwatering Gulab Jamun for dessert.

HOTEL LE GEM,PANIPAT

Hotel Le Gem in Panipat is known for its diverse menu featuring North Indian and Chinese cuisines.The restaurant is praised for its flavorful dishes, fresh ingredients, and prompt service, making it a favorite dining destination in Panipat. DELHI

MOTI MAHAL,DARYAGANJ

Moti Mahal is one of the oldest restaurants in Delhi. It was founded in Daryaganj shortly after we, as a nation, obtained independence. It takes credit for having introduced the recipes for burra kebabs, butter chicken and tandoori chicken to the culinary world. Moti Mahal has over the years expanded to form a chain, with outlets in multiple cities across India.

RAGINDER DA DHABA

Don’t be fooled by its unassuming décor for the food served here is just yum! Delighting foodies around Delhi for over 50 years now, this joint is a legend. It is a paradise for meat lovers as the galawati kebabs here are simply amazing as are butter chicken and tangri kebabs.

KWALITY RESTAURANT,CONNAUGHT PALACE

It has been serving up some amazing dishes in terms of flavour, aroma and presentation for a good 70 years now. Chole bhature is the classic dish to order here while the tandoori pomfret, Kakori kebabs and the mouth-watering taar gosht are the other favourites.

INDIAN ACCENT,LODHI ROAD

Indian Accent Delhi is located inside a small boutique hotel that Chef Manish Mehrotra uses as his abode for fusing local cuisine with contemporary Western elements in imaginative ways. Foie gras-stuffed galawati kebabs with strawberries and green chilli chutney, butter chicken altered with peanut butter and roast peanuts, and crabs with tomato pickle and roasted coconut are all examples of the innovative ideas that Indian Accent has come up with.

KARIM'S,NIZZAMUDIN WEST

This restaurant has been serving the cuisine of the kitchens of the Mughal Empire since 1913. It has its original premises next to the Jama Masjid in Old Delhi which is still the most popular outlet. Its renowned dishes include the mutton burra kebabs and the famous tandoori chicken. The restaurant is a crowd favourite with its no-frills and authentic dining experience.

GOA

MARTIN'S CORNER,BETUL

Martin's Corner is a legendary restaurant located in the village of Betul in South Goa, renowned for its Goan and seafood specialties. With its lively ambiance and friendly service, Martin's Corner is a favorite haunt for both locals and tourists. Try their prawn balchão, crab xec xec, and Goan fish curry for an authentic taste of Goa.

THALASSA,VAGATOR

Thalassa is a Greek restaurant situated on a cliff overlooking the Arabian Sea in Vagator, North Goa. Known for its stunning sunset views and Mediterranean-inspired cuisine, Thalassa offers a unique dining experience. Indulge in dishes like moussaka, souvlaki, and baklava while enjoying live music and panoramic vistas.

MUM'S KITCHEN,PANAGI

Mum's Kitchen is a popular restaurant in the heart of Panaji, the capital city of Goa, specializing in traditional Goan cuisine. GUNPOWDER,ASSAGAON

Gunpowder is a charming restaurant nestled in the village of Assagao in North Goa, known for its innovative take on South Indian cuisine.
