Maharashtra

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an iconic arch-monument in Mumbai, India.

CST Station

CST serves as a major railway hub and symbolizes Mumbai’s heritage.

Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves are a fascinating collection of ancient rock-cut caves in India.

Shivneri Fort, Junnar

It is the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.


Trimbakeshwar Temple, Nashik

It is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Bhimashankar Temple, Pune

The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and attracts pilgrims and trekkers alike.

Aga of Palace, Pune

The headquarters of the Gandhi National Memorial Society.

Raigad Fort

It was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Gujarat

Sabarmati Ashram, Ahmedabad

It was from here that Gandhi led the Dandi March (Salt Satyagraha) on 12 March 1930. Gandhi ji lives here with her wife.

Kankaria Lake, Ahmedabad

It offers attractions like a zoo, toytrain, and water-based rides.The lakefront was revamped in 2007–2008.

ISKCON Temple

is a magnificent structure blending Gujarati Sompura and Rajasthani Khamira architectural styles.

Auto World Vintage Car Museum

Houses over 100 well-preserved vintage cars, including iconic brands like Rolls-Royce, Bentley, and Mercedes-Benz.

Ahmedabad City

India’s first UNESCO World Heritage City.It serves as the administrative headquarters of the Ahmedabad district. This is one of the beautiful city.

Rann of Kutch

The Great Rann of Kutch, a vast salt desert, is one of the largest in the world.It submerges during the rainy season and becomes dry in winter.

The Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity is the world’s tallest statue, standing at an impressive height of 182 meters (597 feet). It is located near Kevadia in the state of Gujarat, India.

Rani ki Vav, Patan

It is a masterpiece of stepwell architecture and sculpture.It features seven levels of stairs adorned with intricate panels depicting religious and secular themes.

Uttarpradesh

Tajmahal, Agra

Agra is home to the iconic Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Along with the Taj Mahal, Agra also houses two other UNESCO World Heritage monuments.

Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri is a deserted fort city that was commissioned by the great Mughal Emperor Akbar in 1569. It was constructed to honor the Sufi saint Sheikh Salim Chisti.

Varanasi

Varanasi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It is deeply rooted in Hindu mythology and culture. This is one of the cultural city.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

It is also known as the Golden Temple, is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple’s location on the banks of the River Ganga adds to its sacred aura.

Mathura and Vrindavan

These twin cities are associated with the birth and early life of Lord Krishna. Mathura is where Lord Krishna was born, while Vrindavan is where he spent his childhood.

Lucknow

The capital city of Uttar Pradesh, Lucknow is renowned for its architectural marvels and cultural heritage. It was a center of art, music, and poetry during the Mughal and Nawabi periods.

Ayodhya

Ayodhya is associated with the birthplace of Lord Rama. The Ram janmabhoomi site and Hanumann Garhi temples major attractions are devotees.It holds immense significance as one of the major holy cities of Hinduism.

Allahabad (Prayagraj)

It is known for the Triveni Sangam, the confluence of the Ganges, Yamuna, and mythical Saraswati rivers. It is situated at the confluence of the Ganges (Ganga) and Yamuna rivers.

Andhrapradesh

Gandikota

Located in the Kadapa district, Gandikota is often referred to as “The Grand Canyon of India.” It lies on the banks of the Pennar River and is home to the Gandikota Fort.

Lepakshi

Situated 14 km east of Hindupur, Lepakshi is a small village in Anantapur district. It showcases the legacy of the Vijayanagara Dynasty.

Amaravati

Amaravati, situated on the banks of the Krishna River, is an ancient city with historical significance.Notable heritage sites include the Amaravati Stupa.

Kurnool

Kurnool, a town with beautiful surroundings, holds much historical value. Key attractions include the Yaganti, Belum Caves, and Rollapadu Wildlife Sanctuary

Chandragiri Fort

The historical fort, built in the 11th century, encircles eight ruined temples dedicated to both Saivite and Vaishnavite pantheons. Which now serves as an archaeological museum.

Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga

Situated in the picturesque Nallamala Hills.Stands alongside the temple of Goddess Bhramaramba, making it doubly auspicious. This is one of the jyotirlinga in the 12 jyotirlinga

Visakhapatnam (Vizag)

One of the oldest port cities in India. Picturesque beaches like Ramakrishna Beach and Rishikonda Beach. Explore the Indira Gandhi Zoological Park to learn about wildlife

Tirupati

Home to the famous Lord Venkateshwara Temple (Tirumala Temple). One of the world’s richest temples and a major pilgrimage center. It is also known as Tirupati Balaji.

Hydrabad

Charminar

This iconic monument, built by the Qutub Shahi Kings, features an impressive structure with eight gates and 87 bastions. Don’t miss the light and sound show narrated by Bollywood superstar Amitabh.

Ramoji Film City

Ramoji Film City is one of the largest film studios globally, spanning 2500 acres. It hosts film units, pre and post-production facilities, and even holds a Guinness World Record.

Statue of Equality

The Statue of Equality honors Sri Ramanujacharya Swamy. It’s a serene monument that reflects the spirit of equality and harmony. It is the one the statue in india.

Golconda Fort

Built by the Qutub Shahi Kings, Golconda Fort boasts impressive architecture, acoustics, and a fascinating water supply system. Explore its history and enjoy the light and sound show.

Hussain Sagar Lake

A large artificial lake in the heart of Hyderabad. Enjoy a boat ride on the lake and admire the massive Buddha statue located on an island in the center.

Chowmahalla Palace

Chowmahalla Palace is one of the many historically significant tourist places in Hyderabad. It was built in Hyderabad between 1857 and 1869 near Charminar.

Hydrabad City

Situated in the Telangana region of India. Often referred to as the “City of Pearls” due to its historical association with diamond and pearl trading.

Snow World

Snow World is one of the main attractions of Hyderabad and is a unique amusement park located in the heart of the city and near Hussain Sagar Lake. Snow world is spread over at an area of 17,000sft.
