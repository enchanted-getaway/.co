MUMBAI

SANJAY GANDHI NATIONAL PARK

Spread over a vast area within the city limits, Sanjay Gandhi National Park is a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. Home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including leopards, deer, and over 250 species of birds, the park offers numerous trails for hiking and exploration. Kanheri Caves, located within the park, provide a glimpse into ancient Buddhist civilization amidst the tranquil surroundings.

MARINE DRIVE

Also known as the Queen's Necklace, Marine Drive is an iconic promenade stretching along the Arabian Sea coastline. Its curved shape, lined with palm trees and streetlights, creates a mesmerizing sight, especially during sunset when the sky paints hues of orange and pink. The sea-facing promenade is a popular spot for leisurely walks, jogging, or simply relaxing while enjoying the sea breeze and panoramic views.

ELEPHANTA CAVES

Situated on Elephanta Island in Mumbai Harbor, the Elephanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for their ancient rock-cut temples and sculptures. Accessible by ferry from the Gateway of India, the caves date back to the 5th to 8th centuries and showcase exquisite artwork depicting Hindu mythology. The island's lush greenery and serene atmosphere add to the mystical charm of this historical site.

ARRAY COLONY

Located in the northern suburbs of Mumbai, Aarey Milk Colony is a sprawling green expanse dotted with dairy farms, forests, and recreational areas. Spanning over 3,000 acres, it serves as the city's 'green lung,' providing residents with a peaceful escape from urban life. Aarey offers nature trails for hiking and cycling, botanical gardens, and picturesque viewpoints overlooking the surrounding hills and valleys. It's an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts seeking solace amidst Mumbai's concrete jungle.

POWAI LAKE

Nestled amidst the bustling streets of Powai, this artificial lake offers a tranquil retreat away from the city's hustle and bustle. Surrounded by verdant hills and landscaped gardens, Powai Lake is a popular destination for picnics, birdwatching, and boating. Visitors can unwind by the lakeside, soak in the scenic beauty, or explore nearby attractions such as Powai Garden and the Powai Waterfall.

HARYANA

YADAVINDRA GARDENS(PINJORE GARDENS)

These historic Mughal-style gardens in Pinjore feature terraced lawns, fountains, and flower beds spread over several levels. The scenic beauty and architectural splendor make it a delightful spot for picnics and leisurely strolls amidst nature's tranquility.

MORNI HILLS

Situated near Panchkula, Morni Hills is the only hill station in Haryana, offering panoramic views of the surrounding plains and valleys. Lush greenery, hiking trails, and a tranquil atmosphere make it a popular weekend getaway for nature lovers.

KALESAR NATIONAL PARK

Located in the Shivalik Hills, Kalesar National Park is renowned for its rich biodiversity and dense forests. Trekking trails, streams, and waterfalls offer opportunities for exploration and adventure amidst pristine wilderness.

SARAJKUNDRA LAKE

Surrounded by the Aravalli Hills, Surajkund Lake is an artificial reservoir known for its picturesque beauty. The annual Surajkund Crafts Mela adds vibrancy to the area, showcasing traditional crafts and cultural performances against the backdrop of the lake and hills.

SULTANPUR NATIONAL PARK

A haven for birdwatchers, Sultanpur National Park is home to over 250 species of resident and migratory birds. Wetlands, woodlands, and grasslands create diverse habitats for wildlife amidst serene surroundings.

DELHI

RIDGE ROAD

The Delhi Ridge, a forested ridge area in the northern part of the city, is home to Ridge Road, which offers scenic drives and walks amidst wooded areas and historical monuments like Mutiny Memorial and Flagstaff Tower.

YAMUNA RIVERFRONT

Despite its pollution issues, the Yamuna Riverfront offers scenic views and recreational spaces along its banks. Parks like Naini Lake and Naini Park provide green oases where visitors can relax and enjoy views of the river.

INDIA GATE & RAJPAT

India Gate, a war memorial, and the adjoining Rajpath form a picturesque vista in central Delhi. The expansive lawns and tree-lined avenues make it a favorite spot for leisurely walks, picnics, and enjoying the evening breeze.

HUMAYUN'S TOMB COMPLEX

This UNESCO World Heritage Site encompasses not only the tomb of Emperor Humayun but also expansive gardens laid out in the Persian Charbagh style. The well-manicured lawns and pathways offer a serene retreat in the heart of the city.

LODHI GARDENS

Spread over 90 acres, Lodhi Gardens is a historical park dotted with ancient tombs, lush gardens, and tranquil water bodies. It's a popular spot for morning walks, picnics, and yoga sessions amidst serene greenery.

PUNJAB

SUKHNA LAKE

Although Chandigarh is a Union Territory, it serves as the capital of both Punjab and Haryana. Sukhna Lake, a man-made reservoir nestled at the foothills of the Shivalik Range, offers scenic views, boating facilities, and walking trails.

GOLDEN TEMPLE COMPLEX

While primarily a religious site, the Golden Temple complex features a serene sarovar (pool) called the Amrit Sarovar. Surrounding the shimmering waters are pristine white marble walkways and historic buildings, creating a peaceful ambiance for visitors.

WAGAH BORDER

The Wagah Border between India and Pakistan is famous for its daily ceremonial closing of the border gates, known as the Wagah Border Ceremony. Visitors can witness the elaborate military drills and flag-lowering ceremony against the backdrop of the picturesque Punjab landscape.

ROPAR WETLAND

Also known as Ropar Lake, this wetland sanctuary in Rupnagar district is a haven for migratory birds, including rare species like the Siberian crane. The serene surroundings and diverse birdlife make it a paradise for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.

ANANDPUR SAHIB

This historic town in Punjab is nestled amid the Sivalik Hills and is known for its religious significance and natural beauty. Surrounded by lush greenery and flowing rivers, Anandpur Sahib offers a peaceful retreat for spiritual seekers and nature lovers alike.

GUJRAT

GIRNAR MOUNTAIN RANGE

The Girnar Mountain Range, located near Junagadh, is a sacred pilgrimage site with several temples and ashrams scattered along its slopes. Visitors can embark on a challenging trek to the summit of Girnar, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

GIR FOREST NATIONAL PARK

Home to the majestic Asiatic lions, Gir Forest National Park is the only place in the world where these lions are found in the wild. The park also features diverse flora and fauna, including leopards, antelopes, and over 300 species of birds.

RANN OF KUTCH

The Rann of Kutch is a vast salt marsh located in the Thar Desert region of Gujarat. During the monsoon season, the marshlands are submerged under water, while in the dry season, the white salt desert stretches as far as the eye can see, creating a surreal landscape.

SAPUTARA HILLS

Situated in the Dang district of Gujarat, Saputara is the state's only hill station, nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Western Ghats. The hill station offers stunning viewpoints, serene lakes, and trekking trails, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

DWARKA BEACH

Located on the Arabian Sea coast, Dwarka Beach offers pristine sandy shores and breathtaking sunset views. The beach is also known for its clear blue waters, making it an ideal spot for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying water sports.

KARNATAKA

WESTERN GHATS

Karnataka is blessed with a significant portion of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's eight "hottest hotspots" of biological diversity. The lush rainforests, rolling hills, and cascading waterfalls of the Western Ghats are ideal for trekking, wildlife spotting, and nature photography.

COASTAL KARNATAKA

The coastal region of Karnataka, stretching along the Arabian Sea, boasts pristine beaches, tranquil backwaters, and lush mangrove forests. Popular coastal destinations include Gokarna, Udupi, and Karwar, where visitors can enjoy sunbathing, water sports, and traditional seafood cuisine.

BANDIPUR NATIONAL PARK

Located in the southern part of Karnataka, Bandipur National Park is one of the premier tiger reserves in India. The park is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and sloth bears, as well as over 200 species of birds. Jeep safaris and nature walks offer opportunities to explore the park's rich biodiversity.

COORG(KODAGU)

Known as the "Scotland of India," Coorg is a picturesque hill station nestled amidst the verdant Western Ghats. Tea and coffee plantations, mist-covered hills, and gushing waterfalls characterize the landscape of Coorg, making it a popular destination for trekking, birdwatching, and romantic getaways.

HAMPI

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hampi is renowned for its ancient ruins, majestic temples, and surreal boulder-strewn landscapes. Situated on the banks of the Tungabhadra River, Hampi offers a unique blend of history and natural beauty, with opportunities for exploration and adventure amidst its rocky terrain.

GOA

DUDHSAGAR WATERFALL

Located in the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary in South Goa, Dudhsagar Waterfalls is one of India's tallest waterfalls. Surrounded by dense forests and cascading over rocky cliffs, the waterfall creates a mesmerizing sight, especially during the monsoon season when it's at its fullest.

COTIGAO WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

Situated in South Goa, Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for nature enthusiasts and birdwatchers. The sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife, including leopards, deer, and langurs, as well as over 200 species of birds. Visitors can explore the sanctuary through guided nature walks and treks.

SALIM ALI BIRD SANCTUARY

Nestled along the Mandovi River in North Goa, the Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary is a haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers. The mangrove forests and wetlands provide habitat for a diverse range of bird species, including kingfishers, herons, and egrets. Boat cruises offer a unique opportunity to spot birds in their natural habitat.

DONA PAULA VIEWPOINT

Offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and the Mandovi River, Dona Paula Viewpoint is a popular spot for enjoying Goa's coastal beauty. The viewpoint is named after Dona Paula de Menezes, a historical figure whose tragic love story adds to the allure of the location.

ARVALEM CAVES & WATERFALL

Located in North Goa, the Arvalem Caves are ancient rock-cut caves believed to date back to the 6th century. Nearby, the Arvalem Waterfall cascades gracefully amidst lush greenery, creating a serene atmosphere. Visitors can explore the caves, enjoy picnics by the waterfall, and immerse themselves in the tranquil surroundings of this hidden gem in Goa.

HYDERABAD

BOTANICAL GARDENS,KONDAPUR

Spanning over 270 acres, these gardens offer a peaceful retreat with their expansive lawns, colorful flower beds, and diverse plant collections. It's a great place for a leisurely stroll or a family outing amidst nature.

MRUGAVANI NATIONAL PARK

Situated on the outskirts of Hyderabad, this wildlife sanctuary is home to various species of animals, birds, and reptiles. Visitors can explore the park through nature trails and enjoy the tranquility of the natural surroundings.

KASU BRAHMANANDA REDDY NATIONAL PARK

Located in the heart of the city, this urban park provides a green escape with its wooded trails, landscaped gardens, and diverse flora and fauna.

DURGAM CHERUVU

Also known as the Secret Lake, Durgam Cheruvu is a serene oasis amidst the bustling city. Surrounded by rocky terrain and lush greenery, it's an ideal spot for picnics, birdwatching, and nature walks.

HUSSAIN SAGAR LAKE

This artificial lake is a prominent landmark in Hyderabad, offering scenic views and recreational activities such as boating and evening strolls along the Tank Bund road.
